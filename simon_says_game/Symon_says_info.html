Simon Says Game: How It Improves Memory and Focus

The Simon Says game is a classic memory-based challenge that tests your ability to remember and repeat patterns correctly. While it looks simple at first, it quickly becomes a powerful exercise for concentration, reaction speed, and cognitive flexibility.

Originally popularized as an electronic memory game in the late 1970s, Simon Says has evolved into many digital versions that continue to train the brain in fun and engaging ways.

The rules are simple:

  • The game shows a sequence of colors.
  • The player must repeat the exact same sequence.

  • The sequence keeps growing until the player makes a mistake.

The challenge increases as the sequence becomes longer, testing your working memory and focus.
